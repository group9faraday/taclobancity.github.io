SUMAN-LATIK



Suman-latik is produced from glutinous rice, coconut milk, brown sugar, or kalamay and is seasoned with a lye solution, giving it a kind of greenish appearance and a distinctive and one-of-a-kind flavor. It's also wrapped in a banana leaf, but some of it is triangular in shape rather than rectangular and some of it is wrapped in hagikhik leaves. Latik, which resembles caramel sauce in appearance, is a syrup made from a combination of coconut milk, kalamay (pure sugar cane, not regular processed sugar), and peanuts that is eventually poured on top of suman. Grated coconut meat is also the best to use as a topping.
